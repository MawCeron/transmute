# Area

Area conversions use square meters (m2) as the SI base unit.

## Metric

Square meters are the standard for everyday measurements. Hectares (ha) and ares (a) are used in land measurement: one hectare is 10,000 m², and one are is 100 m². Square kilometers are used for cities and geographic regions.

## Imperial and US Customary

Square feet (ft2) are the standard for real estate in the United States. Acres (acre) are used in US and UK land transactions: one acre is approximately 4047 m², or about 90% of a standard American football field. Square miles (mi2) cover counties and national parks.

## Nautical

The square nautical mile (nmi2) appears in maritime jurisdictions and exclusive economic zone definitions, where national waters are defined by nautical-mile boundaries.

## Surveying

The rood is a quarter of an acre, once widely used in English land surveying. The US survey township is 36 square miles, used in the Public Land Survey System that divided much of the American Midwest and West.

## Informal Geographic References

Large areas are often described using well-known regions as reference points. Wales (about 20,779 km²) is a classic British reference for large but not enormous areas, while Texas (roughly 696,241 km²) is the go-to American benchmark for something genuinely vast. Journalists and scientists reach for these comparisons when numbers alone feel abstract.
