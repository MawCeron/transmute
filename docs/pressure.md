# Pressure

Pressure conversions use the Pascal (pa) as the SI base unit. One Pascal is one Newton per square meter.

## SI

The Pascal is small for everyday use — standard atmospheric pressure is about 101,325 Pa. Hectopascals (hpa) are the standard unit in meteorology and weather forecasting. Kilopascals (kpa) are used in tire pressure and medical contexts. Megapascals (mpa) and gigapascals (gpa) appear in materials science and geology.

## Bar

The bar (100,000 Pa) is slightly less than one atmosphere and is widely used in industry, scuba diving tables, and European tire pressure gauges. The millibar (mbar) is equivalent to the hectopascal.

## Atmosphere

The standard atmosphere (atm) is defined as exactly 101,325 Pa, representing mean sea-level air pressure. It is a convenient unit for gas chemistry and hyperbaric medicine.

## PSI

Pounds per square inch (psi) is the customary US unit for tire pressure, hydraulics, and compressed gas. 14.696 psi equals one atmosphere. Kilopounds per square inch (ksi) is used in structural engineering.

## Mercury and Water Columns

Millimeters of mercury (mmhg), equivalent to the torr, originate from the classic barometer and remain common in medical blood pressure readings. Inches of mercury (inhg) is standard in US aviation altimetry. Water column units (inh2o, cmh2o) are used for low-pressure measurements in HVAC and spirometry.

## Physiological Curiosities

Everyday pressure phenomena vary widely. A typical human sneeze produces a brief pressure pulse of around 3,000 Pa at the mouth, while an elephant standing on a soft surface concentrates its weight into a surprisingly modest pressure footprint — both are useful mental anchors for the middle of the pressure scale.
