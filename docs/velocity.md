# Velocity (Speed)

Velocity conversions use meters per second (mps) as the SI base unit.

## SI and Metric

Meters per second is the fundamental unit. Kilometers per hour (kmh, also aliased as kph) is the most common unit for road speeds in most of the world. Kilometers per second (kms) is used for stellar velocities and spacecraft speeds.

## Imperial

Miles per hour (mph) is the standard for road speeds in the United States and United Kingdom. Feet per second (fps) appears in ballistics and some engineering contexts.

## Nautical

The knot (kn) is one nautical mile per hour, equal to about 1.852 km/h. It is the universal unit for ship and aircraft speeds when operating under maritime or aviation rules.

## Physics and Reference Speeds

Mach (mach) is the speed of sound at sea level at 15 °C, approximately 340.29 m/s. It varies with altitude, temperature, and humidity, so the Mach number in this tool uses the sea-level standard. The speed of light (c) in vacuum is exactly 299,792,458 m/s by SI definition.

## Biological and Geological Speeds

Biological speeds range across many orders of magnitude. A cheetah reaches about 30 m/s in a sprint. For comparison, continental drift rates — the movement of tectonic plates — are typically measured in centimeters per year, far below anything detectable in everyday experience. Snail locomotion, often cited in discussions of slow movement, sits around 0.013 m/s, which is about 47 m/h. These extremes illustrate how broad the velocity scale truly is.
